Characteristics of the New England, Middle, Chesapeake, and Southern Colonies

New England:
Maritime goods and logging was the main industry due to infirtile soil. They were mainly Puritan.

Chesapeake
They had looser societla conventions and were primarily an agricultual economy.

Middle
The middle colonies were the "colonial breadbasket" because they got that wheat. They had very fertile soil and lots of religious diversity and tolerance.

South
The South was dominated by cash crops and plantations and relied on indentured servitude and later slave labor.
