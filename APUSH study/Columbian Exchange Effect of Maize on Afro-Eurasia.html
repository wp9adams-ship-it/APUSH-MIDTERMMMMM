Columbian Exchange: Effect of Maize on Afro-Eurasia

The Columbian Exchange was the exchange of goods such as plants, animals, diseases, people, culture, technology, and more between the old and the new world. Maize(corn) was one of the most significant crops as it led to a massive population growth within the Native community allowing them to build structured and sophisticated societies. Maize also had a multitude of effects in the New World like in Afro-Eurasia (Africa, Europe, and Asia).

Introduction to Maize

Maize (corn) spread rapidly throughout the Americas and became the staple crop of many people. Maize led to the creation of larger and more complex socities within the new world, such as the Aztecs. Societies that would adopt the cultivation of Maize would see 4 main effects. The 1st one is economic development. Because Native communities who adopted Maize saw population growth, the larger socities led to more trade with other larger socities. More food leads to a greater population which leads to a larger labor force which leads to more specialized labor and different types of labor, some relating to art. Societies that adopted Maize typically also became permanent settlements instead of being hunter-gatherers who were constantly changing living locations. More complex social and political structures form when people are located in one place and not constantly moving. In short, they became sedentary. They also developed advanced irrigation techniques and social diversification, Labor became more specialized and social hierarchies developed.

Introduction of Maize to Afro-Eurasia

Maize was domesticated in Mesoamerica thousands of years before Europe made contact. After 1492 when Columbus made contact, trade rapidly spread. Basically it just expanded the population. The end.
