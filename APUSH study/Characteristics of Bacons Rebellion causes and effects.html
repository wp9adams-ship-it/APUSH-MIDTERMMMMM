Tensions between Natives and Vurginians grew as Virginian colonists continued to encroach on Native land and take theire valuable resources. William Berkely was the current governor of the Jamestown colony and was percieved to have a tyrannical rule over the colony, and was hated for being too nice and leniant towards the Natives. Discontent among indentured servants comtinued to grow as conditions continued to get worse/remain the same. Nathaniel Bacon led a rebellion against Governor Berkeley with a group of indentured servants. They pushed William Berkeley out of Jamestown and burnt the city. The rebellion was put down and the elite, worried about more indentured servant revolts, slowly shifted the labor system to slave labor (chattel slavery).
