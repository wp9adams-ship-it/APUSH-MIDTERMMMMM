City on a Hill

Puritan leader John Winthrop went with his gang to Massachusetts as a model Christain community and a beacon of righteousness. He called it a city on a hill. The colonists were to build a perfect, visible Christian society, a shining example (a "light to the world") for a morally corrupt England and the world to see. John Winthrop, governor of the Massachusetts Bay Colony, used the phrase in his sermon.
